Introduction

This document describes the mappings from the Missy 3 data models to different versions of the Data Documentation Initiative social science metadata standard.

  • generic example XML documents
    • DDI 3.2

DDIInstance

URN (DDI 3.2 )
urn:ddi:<agency ID>:<UUID>:<release version>.<minor version>

Series

XPath Expression ( DDI 3.2 )
/DDIInstance/g:Group[@time='T2']

/DDIInstance/g:Group/r:Coverage/r:SpatialCoverage/r:Country
--> geographical coverage
  • geographical coverage
    • contains all countries of series
  • @time
    • Indicates how all members of the group are related along the dimension of time
      • "T0" No specified relationship
      • "T1" Single Occurrence
      • "T2" Multiple Occurrence: Regular Occurrence: Continuing
      • "T3" Multiple Occurrence: Regular Occurrence: Limited time
      • "T4" Multiple Occurrence: Irregular Occurrence: Continuing
      • "T5" Multiple Occurrence: Irregular Occurrence: Limited time
  • links to official DDI documentation of the DDI Alliance
    • DDI 3.2

Mappings

Descriptions

field description
title The title of the series in English
subtitle Subtitle of the series in English.
release year The year in which data were released irregardless of when data were collected.
version number The version number serves to distinguish multiple releases within one year from another.
data publisher The legal entity responsible for authoring the data i.e. EUROSTAT
abstract A short summary describing the purpose, nature, and scope of the series.
keywords You can choose multiple keywords to describe the series.
geographical coverage Information on the geographic coverage of the series. Includes the total geographic scope of the data. As the list of included countries will be computed automatically from the country specific information you do not have to list them out here.
organization Details the organisation of the series and the responsibilities of involved parties.
universe Definition of the universe.
sampling Details requirements regarding sampling.
data collection Details requirements regarding data collection.
anonymization Details the anonymisation procedures.
legal basis Detail the legal norms on the basis of which the series is carried out.
access conditions Details the access conditions as issued by the data provider.
access form Links to an (online)access form, with which data can be ordered.
access contact Contact information regarding data access. Reference to specific persons should be avoided here and an institutional e-mail address should be provided.
comparability over time Details breaks in continuity or methodology over time.
comparability over space Details differences in data or data collection between countries.
notes Provide here any further pertinent information regarding the series that cannot be included in any other field.
citation List all sources used for the documentation at the series level here. Please provide information on: Author (usually Institution) Title Year of publication Link to document (if available)

Studies

Studies with Multiple Countries

The studies 'EU-SILC 2009' and 'EU-LFS 2010' are divided into multiple countries.

Studies (e.g. EU-SILC 2011) are represented as sub-groups of series (e.g. EU-SILC)

XPath Expression ( DDI 3.2 )
/DDIInstance/g:Group/g:SubGroup[@geography='G4']

/DDIInstance/g:Group/g:SubGroup/r:Coverage/r:TemporalCoverage/r:ReferenceDate/r:SimpleDate
--> temporal coverage
  • @geography
    • Indicates how all members of the sub-group are related along the dimension of geography.
      • "G0" No specified relationship
      • "G1" Single geography surveyed multiple times
      • "G2" Single geography surveyed once
      • "G3" Rolling geography
      • "G4" Different geography each survey
  • links to official DDI documentation of the DDI Alliance
    • DDI 3.2

Mappings ( Study: General Description )

Descriptions
field description
title The title of the study in English and its official date
subtitle Subtitle of the study
abstract Provide here any information that is pertinent to the specific study but not the series. For example you should mention here any ad-hoc modules implemented in the current study or alterations in study design. This information should be complementary to the information provided in the abstract at the series level.
keywords In addition to keywords at the series level you can assign additional keywords here which are specific to a given study (e.g. as they refer to an ad-hoc module).
geographical coverage Information on the geographic coverage of the study. Includes the total geographic scope of the data. As the list of included countries will be computed automatically from the country specific information you do not have to list them out here.
time period covered The time period the data refer to. This can be different points in time if for example retrospective questions are asked.
notes Provide any information on the study here that you feel is important for researchers and that cannot be included in any other field
citation List all sources used for the documentation at the series level here. Please provide information on: Author (usually Institution) Title Year of publication Link to document (if available)

Mappings ( Study: Country-Specific Information )

Descriptions
field description
corresponding national study Provide here the alternative title of the study in the national context. If the study is carried out or published under a different title by the respective NSI provide the appropriate title here.
producer The legal entity/entities responsible for carrying out the study. Normally this will be the national statistical institutes.
universe Definition of the universe.
Target Sample Size (cross-sectional) - sampling units Sampling units used during sampling
Target Sample Size (cross-sectional) - minimum effective sample size The minimum sample size required for this country. Usually defined as a requirement by Eurostat.
Target Sample Size (cross-sectional) - actual sample size Size of the drawn sample. Musst be larger or equal to the effective sample size.
Target Sample Size (cross-sectional) - achieved sample size Number of interviews realized.
Target Sample Size (cross-sectional) - size of design effect The design effect corrects for bias that results from applying complex sampling criteria (in comparison to a simple random sample).
Target Sample Size (cross-sectional) - effective sample size Is calculted by dividing the achieved sample size through the design effect.
Target Sample Size (longitudinal) - sampling units Sampling units used during sampling
Target Sample Size (longitudinal) - minimum effective sample size Requirments regarding the effective sample size in a country.
Target Sample Size (longitudinal) - actual sample size
Target Sample Size (longitudinal) - achieved sample size Number of interviews realized
Target Sample Size (longitudinal) - size of design effect The design effect corrects for bias that results from applying complex sampling criteria (in comparison to a simple random sample)
Target Sample Size (longitudinal) - effective sample size Is calculted by dividing the achieved sample size through the design effect.
Target Sample Size (ad-hoc Module) - sampling units Sampling units used during sampling
Target Sample Size (ad-hoc Module) - minimum effective sample size Requirments regarding the effective sample size in a country.
Target Sample Size (ad-hoc Module) - actual sample size Size of the realized sample. Musst be larger or equal to the effective sample size.
Target Sample Size (ad-hoc Module) - achieved sample size Number of interviews realized
Target Sample Size (ad-hoc Module) - size of design effect The design effect corrects for bias that results from applying complex sampling criteria (in comparison to a simple random sample)
Target Sample Size (ad-hoc Module) - effective sample size Is calculted by dividing the achieved sample size through the design effect.
available data Provides information on the different data types which are availble for a given year.
sampling procedure - participation mandatory Information on whether participation is compulsory or not.
sampling procedure - source of sampling frame Basis for drawing the sample. For example this might include census data or registers.
sampling procedure - sampling design Specific information on the sampling design.
sampling procedure - primary sampling units In the case of a multi stage sample this field provides information on which sampling units are selected in the first step.
sampling procedure - secondary sampling units If a multi stage sample was used this field provides information on which sampling units are selected in the second step.
sampling procedure - stratification criteria If a stratified sample was used you should detail stratification criteria here.
sampling procedure - sampling method Additional information regarding sampling methodology that are not provided in the other fields. For example this could include special cases such as the represenativeness in regards to small subpopuations (e.g. over sea teritories)
number of rotational groups Number of rotational groups. This also provides information on how often respondents are questioned in the panel.
units of observation Describes the level at which data is collected. Selecting multiple options is possible.
dates of data collection - start date Timeframe in which data were collected.
dates of data collection - end date Timeframe in which data were collected.
mode of data collection - type of data source Information from what kind of sources data were collected.
mode of data collection - interview mode (PAPI) "Includes information on the different data collection modes used in the survey. Note that the sum of all fields must add up to 1000%. Please use a full stop to separate decimals. The following options exist: PAPI: PaperAssistet Personal Interview CAPI: ComputerAssisted Personal Interview CATI: ComputerAssisted Telefon Interview Self-Administered: Self Administered Questionaire which is filled out without the assistance of an interviewer Websurvey: Survey filled out online without the assistance of an interviewer"
mode of data collection - interview mode (CAPI) "Includes information on the different data collection modes used in the survey. Note that the sum of all fields must add up to 1000%. Please use a full stop to separate decimals. The following options exist: PAPI: PaperAssistet Personal Interview CAPI: ComputerAssisted Personal Interview CATI: ComputerAssisted Telefon Interview Self-Administered: Self Administered Questionaire which is filled out without the assistance of an interviewer Websurvey: Survey filled out online without the assistance of an interviewer"
mode of data collection - interview mode (CATI) "Includes information on the different data collection modes used in the survey. Note that the sum of all fields must add up to 1000%. Please use a full stop to separate decimals. The following options exist: PAPI: PaperAssistet Personal Interview CAPI: ComputerAssisted Personal Interview CATI: ComputerAssisted Telefon Interview Self-Administered: Self Administered Questionaire which is filled out without the assistance of an interviewer Websurvey: Survey filled out online without the assistance of an interviewer"
mode of data collection - interview mode (self-administered) "Includes information on the different data collection modes used in the survey. Note that the sum of all fields must add up to 1000%. Please use a full stop to separate decimals. The following options exist: PAPI: PaperAssistet Personal Interview CAPI: ComputerAssisted Personal Interview CATI: ComputerAssisted Telefon Interview Self-Administered: Self Administered Questionaire which is filled out without the assistance of an interviewer Websurvey: Survey filled out online without the assistance of an interviewer"
mode of data collection - interview mode (WebSurvey) "Includes information on the different data collection modes used in the survey. Note that the sum of all fields must add up to 1000%. Please use a full stop to separate decimals. The following options exist: PAPI: PaperAssistet Personal Interview CAPI: ComputerAssisted Personal Interview CATI: ComputerAssisted Telefon Interview Self-Administered: Self Administered Questionaire which is filled out without the assistance of an interviewer Websurvey: Survey filled out online without the assistance of an interviewer"
mode of data collection - percentage of proxy interviews Percentage of interviews that were not filled in with information provided by the respondent personally but by a representative.
weighting - Initial Design Weight: Target The initial design weights are used to assess the representativeness of the sample in regards to the universe. This field notes what level these initial design weights refer to.
weighting - Initial Design Weight: Method Method on the base of which the initial design weighting is carried out.
weighting - Initial Non-Response: Method Description of procedures undertaken to correct for bias from systematic non response.
weighting - Initial Calibration: Method Description of calibration procedures undertaken to increase consistency with existing population statistics.
weighting - Weighting: Method Information on methodology regarding weighting
notes Additional important country specific information not provided in any other field.
citation List all sources used for the documentation at the series level here. Please provide information on: Author (usually Institution) Title Year of publication Link to document (if available)

Comparison

Variables are comparable over multiple years, if they are assigned to the same topical concept using the thematic classification. The Comparison Module is used to implement comparison of variables.

  • further description

Die thematische Gliederung (dargestellt durch DDI Concepts) ist die zentrale Basis des Vergleichs. Man knnte noch zustzliche Informationen auf Ebene der Fragen oder Variablen festhalten in Comparison, wo ein Itemvergleich mglich ist. Es kann beschrieben werden, ob etwas identisch ist oder nur hnlich, eine Beschreibung der hnlichkeit oder Verschiedenheit und auch eine numerische Darstellung davon. Dies ist nur dann wichtig, wenn Informationen fr Zwecke der Vergleichbarkeit zustzlich zur thematischen Gliederung angeboten werden sollen; z.B. wenn Variablen aus zwei verschiedenen Jahren Bezug zum gleichen Concept haben, aber nicht total identisch sind.
